How to Read ASL Gloss


ASL glossing involves the meticulous representation of each sign using its English equivalent. This word-for-word approach ensures that the meaning and structure of ASL sentences are preserved in written form. For instance, the ASL sign for "dog" is succinctly glossed as "DOG," maintaining the direct correspondence between sign and written representation.

"Glossing is the bridge between silence and understanding, transforming the fluidity of sign language into the permanence of written form."

Central to ASL communication are non-manual markers, encompassing facial expressions, body movements, and spatial referencing, which convey grammatical nuances and contextual information. Within glossing, these non-manual markers are encapsulated through symbols and annotations, enriching the written representation of ASL discourse. For example, the furrowing of eyebrows may denote a question, while a raised brow could signify emphasis or surprise, enhancing the depth and clarity of ASL glossing.

Glossing Conventions

Glossing conventions encompass a diverse array of symbols and notations designed to capture the multifaceted nature of ASL grammar and syntax. These conventions serve as guidelines for representing various linguistic elements within ASL discourse. Symbols such as "+" signify the repetition of a sign for emphasis, while "!" denotes increased intensity or vigor in signing. Additionally, the "#" symbol is utilized to represent lexicalized fingerspelled words, showcasing instances where fingerspelling has become ingrained within ASL lexicon. For instance, "#ALL" or "#WHAT" exemplify lexicalized fingerspelling, where individual letters form a cohesive sign.

Grammatical Elements

Glossing extends beyond the representation of individual signs to encompass grammatical elements such as pronouns, verbs, and classifiers. Terms like "PRO.1" denote first-person pronouns like "I" or "me," while classifiers, integral to ASL discourse, are represented through specialized glossing conventions. By meticulously adhering to these conventions, glossing facilitates the accurate transcription and analysis of ASL discourse, empowering researchers, educators, and learners to engage with the richness and complexity of ASL communication.

In essence, ASL glossing serves as a vital tool for promoting inclusivity, understanding, and appreciation of ASL as a distinct and vibrant language. Through its meticulous representation of ASL discourse, glossing fosters accessibility and comprehension, bridging the gap between visual and written language modalities and enriching the linguistic landscape for all.
